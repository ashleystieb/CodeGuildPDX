Katherine Johnson

Introduction

Katherine Johnson was born in 1918. She was the youngest of four children. Her father was a farmer and her mother was a teacher. She graduated from high school at 14 and entered West Virginia State College, which was a historically black college. She took every math class offered. She graduated in 1937 at age 18 with a degree in Mathematics and French.

The first jobs she found were teaching jobs until she heard that NASA hired African American mathematicians in their Guidance and Navigation Department. She was offered a job in 1953. From 1953 until 1958 she worked as a "computer" and analyzed topics. In 1958 NASA was desegregated and she worked as an aerospace technologist. She calculated the trajectory of the 1961 space flight for, Alan Shepard the first American in space. She also calculated the launch window for his 1961 Mercury Mission.
